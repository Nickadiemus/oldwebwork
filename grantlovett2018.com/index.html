Platform

The University of Kentucky is a thriving institution, but like every great university, it is constantly facing new challenges as faculty, students, and the community adapt to our ever-evolving world. We are in the wake of massive budget cuts from the state legislature, a student body that is trying to survive the rising costs of a college education, and an administration that is still working towards providing equal opportunity to all students on campus.

We are Riley Grant and Tucker Lovett and we are running to be your Student Body President and Vice President so we can foster real change that will help our University overcome these challenges. Our platform is based on renewing progress in the areas of student and equal opportunities, initiating needed reform to protect students, and electing leaders you can trust. We will work with administration to make sure there are programs in place to protect students on and off campus. This includes the expansion of the Kentucky Wildcab program, prompting a discussion on a dated University alcohol policy, and a more intense focus on making mental health treatment easier for students to access.

We will work to no end with the administration to identify and eliminate student costs. This starts with SGA, which has had surplus funds for the past three years. We plan to cut the SGA budget by almost 20% to enable other organizations and students to get the funding they need. Further, we plan to redistribute these excess funds through midsized funding grants to increase opportunities for other campus organizations. Our plan includes improving the efficiency and effectiveness of SGA programs such as Wildcat Wardrobe, Student Legal Services, Childcare Grants, the Government Internship Program, and the Student coalition for diversity and inclusion. Beyond these programs we have ideas for improving the first-year experience, continuing lobbying efforts in Frankfort, and amplifying the student voice on the Board of Trustees.
